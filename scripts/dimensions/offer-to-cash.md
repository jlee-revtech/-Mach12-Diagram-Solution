# Offer-to-Cash in a US Government Contractor: The Eight-Dimension Operating Profile

Offer-to-Cash (O2C) at a GovCon aerospace and defense company is the sell-side value stream: capture, proposal, negotiation, award, contract setup, funding, execution, billing, revenue recognition, modifications, and closeout. It is not the commercial "order-to-cash" of a consumer company. The customer is the US Government, the price is often built up from cost, the invoice format is prescribed by regulation, and the money does not move until data flows correctly through government portals. On SAP S/4HANA the stream runs on SD, SD-BIL, RAR, resource-related billing (DP91/DP96), and PS, typically with the Dassian A&D add-on providing Contracts (CLIN/SLIN/ACRN structure, modifications, DD250), PBP, Billing (BIL/DRB), ABS, and Results Analysis (RAENH). Everything below assumes that stack.

## People

The consultant's first job is to map who actually owns each decision. Titles vary by company size; a defense OEM prime has all of these as separate people, a Tier-2 supplier may combine three of them into one person.

| Role | Owns | Signs off on | Day-to-day pains |
|---|---|---|---|
| VP Contracts (or Director of Contracts) | Contract portfolio risk, negotiation strategy, delegation of signature authority | TINA certificates, contract execution, UCA acceptance, major mod signatures | Definitization backlog, mod-to-master drift, understaffed CA team, audit findings |
| Contracts Manager / Contract Administrator (CA) | Individual contract files, mods, funding tracking, LOF/LOC notices, correspondence with PCO/ACO | Mod entry into the system, funding acceptance, contractual notices | Reading SF30 PDFs and re-keying them, chasing funding docs, keeping the contract brief current |
| Pricing Director and Estimators | Cost volume build-up, rate application, estimating system compliance, sweeps | Basis of estimate, price proposal release, sweep completion memo | ProPricer-to-SAP rate disconnects, last-minute BOE changes, defective pricing exposure |
| Capture Manager / Proposal Manager | Pipeline, bid/no-bid, Sections L and M compliance, color team reviews | Proposal submission package | Compliance matrix churn, data calls into engineering, pipeline data living in a CRM nobody reconciles to backlog |
| Billing Specialist (Government Billing) | Invoice and voucher preparation, WAWF/PIEE submission, rejections, progress payment requests | Invoice release into WAWF (under a certifying official) | WAWF rejections for DoDAAC or CLIN mismatches, rate table mismatches, unbilled aging explanations |
| Revenue Accountant / Revenue Manager | ASC 606 POB model, RAR postings, EAC-driven POC revenue, loss provisions | Monthly revenue close, POB structure changes, contract cost-to-cost inputs | EAC timing from program control, RAR reprocessing errors, explaining unbilled to auditors |
| Controller / Assistant Controller | AR, unbilled, DSO, close calendar, external audit interface | Billed/unbilled reconciliation, journal entries, reserve adequacy | Unbilled growth with no owner, retroactive rate adjustments, quarter-end billing pushes |
| Program Manager (consumer of this stream) | Execution, EAC, funding burn awareness | EAC used for revenue, LOF projections | Learning about funding exhaustion too late, mods stuck in contracts |
| Compliance / Government Accounting Director | Business system adequacy, CAS disclosure statement, ICS, DCAA/DCMA relationship | Incurred cost submission, provisional billing rates, disclosure statement changes | Fragmented data pulls for audits, rate variance true-ups |

Typical reporting lines: Contracts reports to the General Counsel or directly to the CEO/COO at primes (deliberately independent of programs); Pricing usually under the CFO or under Business Development; Billing and Revenue under the Controller; Compliance under the CFO. Capture/Proposal under a BD executive.

Who must be in the room: contract structure and mod-handling decisions need the VP Contracts plus a working CA; billing design needs the Billing Specialist lead plus the Controller plus somebody who has personally cleared WAWF rejections; RAR/POB design needs the Revenue Manager plus program control (the EAC seam); anything touching rates needs Pricing and Compliance together. Do not design CLIN structures with only IT present.

## Process

The lifecycle: capture (pipeline, bid/no-bid), proposal (BOE, cost volume, compliance matrix), negotiation (fact-finding, sweeps, handshake), award, contract setup (master data in SD/Dassian/PS), funding (ACRN-level obligations), execution, billing, revenue, modifications, and closeout. Each stage has a control point where the government can later find you noncompliant, so treat each as an audit boundary, not just a workflow step.

**Pricing and TINA.** Above the certified cost or pricing data threshold (FAR 15.403-4, absent an exception such as adequate price competition or commercial product) the contractor certifies that cost or pricing data were accurate, complete, and current as of the date of price agreement, or as of an earlier cutoff date agreed between the parties and stated on the certificate (FAR 15.406-2); negotiating that cutoff date is part of sweep planning because it bounds the sweep window and the defective-pricing exposure period. The threshold is not a constant and must be looked up against the contract award date: 2 million dollars for contracts awarded July 1, 2018 through September 30, 2025; 2.5 million dollars effective October 1, 2025 under the FAR 1.109 five-year inflation adjustment (which per FAR 1.109(d) also flows the adjusted threshold into existing contract clauses); and 10 million dollars for contracts entered into after June 30, 2026 under FY2026 NDAA Section 1804(c), with the FAR text typically lagging the statute, so expect class deviations in the interim. Because the CAS applicability threshold is tied to the TINA threshold, CAS coverage advice moves with it. The operational consequence is the sweep: in the days between handshake and certificate signature, estimating and supply chain sweep for newer vendor quotes, updated actuals, rate changes, and make/buy shifts, and disclose anything found. Defective pricing (a post-award DCAA audit finding that data was not current) produces a price reduction plus interest, so the sweep log is a real artifact. This ties directly to the estimating system: DFARS 252.215-7002 defines estimating system adequacy criteria (consistent estimating practices, documented BOEs, use of current rates, management review), and CAS 401 requires that you estimate, accumulate, and report costs consistently, meaning the proposal cost structure must map to the WBS/cost-collection structure you will execute in SAP PS.

**Contract types and risk posture.** A practitioner reads the contract type before anything else because it determines billing instrument, revenue method, and who eats overruns: FFP (contractor risk, invoice on delivery or milestones, possibly progress payments); FPIF (shared risk via share ratio to the PTA, then FFP behavior above it); CPFF (government cost risk, fee fixed); CPIF/CPAF (fee moves with cost or award-fee determinations); T&M/LH (fixed loaded hourly rates plus materials at cost, a ceiling, and a withhold); IDIQ vehicles where the base contract carries terms and ceilings but the money and work live on task/delivery orders (each order is effectively its own contract in the system); OTAs (other transactions outside FAR, negotiated terms, often milestone-funded); and letter contracts/UCAs, where work starts against a not-to-exceed before terms are definitized. UCAs carry a definitization schedule (DFARS 217.74 governs; obligations are limited to a fraction of the NTE until definitization), and a definitization backlog is one of the first health metrics to pull at any client.

Contract type quick reference (billing instrument and revenue behavior follow the type):

| Type | Primary billing instrument | Revenue pattern | Watch items |
|---|---|---|---|
| FFP | Delivery/milestone invoice via WAWF after DD250 acceptance; SF1443 progress payments if authorized | Over time cost-to-cost on most defense production (no alternative use, right to payment); otherwise point in time at acceptance | Loss provisions surface here first; progress payment liquidation math |
| FPIF | Progress payments; final price settles via share ratio up to the PTA/ceiling | Cost-to-cost with incentive fee estimated as constrained variable consideration | Share line and PTA modeling; EAC swings move fee |
| CPFF | SF1034/1035 cost vouchers at provisional billing rates | Cost plus fee recognized as costs are incurred | Fee withhold under FAR 52.216-8; LOF monitoring is constant |
| CPIF / CPAF | Cost vouchers; fee per incentive formula or award-fee determination | Incentive/award fee as constrained variable consideration | Award-fee accrual policy must be documented and consistent |
| T&M / LH | Vouchers at fixed loaded hourly rates plus materials at cost | Over time; right-to-invoice expedient often fits | Ceiling management; payment withhold under FAR 52.232-7 (5 percent up to 50,000 dollars) |
| IDIQ task/delivery orders | Per order, inheriting order type | Per order; the IDIQ base itself usually carries no revenue until orders issue | Guaranteed minimum; ordering period; order-level setup discipline |
| OTA | Milestone payments per negotiated terms | Per agreement terms; analyze, do not assume FAR patterns | Non-FAR instrument; clause library does not apply wholesale |
| Letter contract / UCA | Per NTE and interim terms until definitized | Constrained until definitization settles price | Obligation limits before definitization; the definitization clock |

**Contract structure.** DFARS 204.71 prescribes the line item scheme: four-digit CLINs (0001), six-character SLINs where alpha suffixes (0001AA) are separately identified/funded and numeric suffixes (000101) are informational, and ELINs on exhibits (typically CDRLs on a DD1423). ACRNs (two-character codes, AA, AB, ...) tie funding lines of accounting to line items; a single SLIN can be funded by multiple ACRNs and billing must consume funding in the payment instructions' prescribed order. Incremental funding is the norm on cost-type work: the contract value and the funded value diverge, and the CA monitors burn against funding under FAR 52.232-20 (Limitation of Cost, fully funded) or 52.232-22 (Limitation of Funds, incrementally funded), issuing the notification when costs approach the clause threshold (commonly 75 percent). In SAP this means funded value must exist as structured data at the ACRN/SLIN level (Dassian Contracts carries this; plain SD does not), and the LOF report must run against actuals plus commitments, not just billed.

**Modifications.** Mods arrive on SF30. Distinguish administrative (unilateral, e.g. paying office change), unilateral (e.g. exercise of option, change order under the Changes clause), and bilateral (supplemental agreements requiring signature). Separately distinguish funding mods (add money to existing scope, ACRN changes) from scope mods (change the work, may trigger REA/claims and new TINA certification if over threshold). The chronic failure is mod-to-master synchronization: the conformed contract exists as PDFs in EDA and in the CA's contract brief, while the system of execution (SD contract, Dassian contract master, billing plans, PS budgets, RAR POBs) drifts. Dassian's mod object versions the CLIN structure per mod, which is the reason to insist mods are entered as mods, never as direct edits to the contract master.

**Billing by contract type.** FFP hardware: DD250 (Material Inspection and Receiving Report, DFARS Appendix F) acceptance drives the invoice, submitted through WAWF; large FFP/FPIF production contracts often carry progress payments under FAR 52.232-16, requested on SF1443 at the customary rate (80 percent of incurred cost for large business), liquidated against delivery invoices. Performance-based payments (FAR subpart 32.10) replace progress payments on some fixed-price work: event-driven, capped at 90 percent of price, and administered in Dassian PBP. Cost-type and T&M work bills via cost vouchers (SF1034 with SF1035 continuation) using provisional billing rates; interim vouchers route through DCAA (contractors admitted to direct billing submit straight to the paying office with DCAA sampling), and fixed fee is typically subject to a withhold under FAR 52.216-8 (up to 15 percent of fee or 100,000 dollars, whichever is less). Milestone billing serves FFP services and OTAs. Every payment request routes in WAWF/PIEE by the DoDAACs on the contract (admin office, inspect/accept, ship-to, paying office); wrong DoDAAC routing is the top rejection cause, so DoDAAC data quality is a billing design topic, not a data cleanup afterthought.

**Business systems and audit.** This stream owns or feeds several of the six DFARS business systems (DFARS 252.242-7005 gives the withhold mechanism: 5 percent of amounts due per covered system with a significant deficiency, reduced to 2 percent once the contracting officer accepts an acceptable corrective action plan, capped at 10 percent cumulative across multiple deficient systems, and applied against interim payments such as progress payments, PBPs, and interim cost vouchers, not final payments): the estimating system (252.215-7002) is owned here; the accounting system (252.242-7006) is fed by billing and rates; EVMS and MMAS belong to other streams but consume this stream's contract structure. DCAA walks through: voucher support (timesheets to labor distribution to voucher), provisional vs actual rate application, TINA/defective pricing files (sweep evidence), the incurred cost submission, and unbilled composition. DCMA (usually through the ACO) walks through: contract file completeness, progress payment administration, business system CAPs, and definitization status. When they audit billing they trace one voucher end to end; the consultant's test of a billing design is exactly that trace.

**Revenue (ASC 606).** Performance obligations are identified per contract (often one POB per contract or per CLIN group on production; distinct POBs for development vs production vs sustainment). Most defense work recognizes revenue over time using cost-to-cost percentage of completion, which makes revenue a function of the EAC: revenue = POC x transaction price, POC = actual cost / EAC cost. This is the EAC seam with Plan-to-Perform: program control owns the EAC, revenue accounting consumes it, and the close calendar must sequence EAC lock before the revenue run. Loss provisions are booked in full in the period a contract-level loss becomes evident. In S/4 this runs in RAR (POBs in FARR_D_POB, revenue postings from the RAI monitor) or, in Dassian-centric designs, RAENH performs POB-level results analysis in CO-PS (hooking classic RA via the results analysis customer exit) and feeds the postings; pick one primary engine and make the other read-only, never both posting.

**Closeout.** Physical completion starts the clock, but final invoicing on cost-type contracts depends on final indirect rates: the incurred cost submission (due six months after fiscal year end under FAR 52.216-7), DCAA audit or low-risk sampling, ACO rate agreement, then the completion voucher (due within 120 days after final rate settlement), release of withheld fee, deobligation of excess funds, and government property/patent/classified closeout steps. Quick closeout (FAR 42.708) is the lever for small residual-value contracts. Closeout backlog is unglamorous and always worse than the client admits; it ties up funded backlog reporting and audit attention.

## Technology and Systems

**SAP core for this stream:**

| Component | Role in O2C | Key objects |
|---|---|---|
| SD | Sales contract as the execution master; task orders as contracts or orders | VA41/VA42, VBAK/VBAP, item categories, copy control |
| SD-BIL | Billing documents, milestone billing plans, outputs | VF01/VF04, FPLA/FPLT billing plans, VBRK/VBRP |
| RAR | ASC 606 revenue: POBs, allocation, over-time recognition | FARR_D_POB, RAI monitor, revenue postings to ACDOCA |
| RRB (DP91/DP96) | Resource-related billing: converts WBS-assigned PS actuals to debit memo requests on cost-type and T&M work (DP91 single / DP96 collective against sales documents; DP90 is the sibling transaction for CS/PM service orders) | DIP profile (ODP1), sales document flow |
| PS | WBS as cost collection and billing element; RA; settlement | CJ20N, PROJ/PRPS, KKA2 results analysis |

**Dassian add-on:** Contracts provides the government contract master (CLIN/SLIN/ACRN tree, funding at ACRN level, mod versioning, DD250 generation, flowdown clause tracking) sitting on top of the SD contract; Billing (BIL with DRB rate logic) generates cost-based invoices and vouchers with government formats (SF1034/1035 content, progress payment support) from PS actuals and rate decks; ABS (Acceptance Billing Summary) manages the DD250/acceptance-to-invoice linkage; PBP administers performance-based payment events and liquidation; RAENH provides POB-level results analysis and rev-rec inside CO-PS as an alternative or complement to RAR. Decision rule: if the client runs cost-type and incrementally funded work at any scale, plain SD contracts are inadequate; the choice is Dassian (or an equivalent A&D layer) vs heavy custom, and custom always underestimates mods and funding.

**Surrounding non-SAP systems typically found:** ProPricer (proposal pricing, the de facto standard; TruePlanning or in-house Excel models also appear), Salesforce or Deltek GovWin IQ for capture/pipeline, Unanet or Deltek Costpoint at acquired subsidiaries (frequent coexistence problem), a contract lifecycle tool (often SharePoint plus the CA's contract brief; sometimes Icertis or Agiloft), Costpoint or SAP itself for the ICE model feed (the DCAA incurred cost submission workbook), and document management holding conformed contracts. Keep/retire/integrate guidance: keep ProPricer, integrate its rate tables with SAP costing rates one-directionally (SAP rates are master); retire spreadsheet funding trackers into Dassian funding objects; integrate the CRM pipeline to backlog reporting but never let CRM numbers into the financial backlog; retire legacy billing formatters once Dassian BIL output is validated against real paid vouchers.

Per-topic gotchas on the SAP side: resource-related billing (DP91/DP96 for sales-document/project-based billing; DP90 for service orders) depends entirely on the DIP profile's material determination and source selection, maintained in ODP1; get cost element groupings wrong and cost-type invoices misclassify labor vs ODCs, which DCAA will find in a voucher trace. Milestone billing plans (FPLT) must have their billing blocks released by an acceptance event, not by hand, or you lose the DD250-to-invoice control. RAR contract combination and POB determination rules should be decided before contract masters load, because re-deriving POBs on live contracts is painful reprocessing. And SD copy control from contract to order to billing document is where CLIN identity gets silently lost; test that the CLIN/SLIN reference survives to the invoice line and into the WAWF payload.

**Government portals at the boundary:** SAM.gov (entity registration, exclusions, and solicitation posting; source for opportunity intake), PIEE/WAWF (all DoD payment requests and receiving reports, DD250s, progress payment requests), EDA (Electronic Data Access: conformed contracts and mods, the authoritative source the mod-sync process reads from), FPDS-NG (award data), CPARS (past performance), eSRS (subcontract reporting), and the DCAA/DCMA interfaces which are human plus document based. Automate the WAWF boundary via file-based FTP/EDI submission where volume justifies it; keep a human certifying official in the release step regardless.

**Migration objects and load sequencing** for a brownfield or Costpoint-to-S/4 move: (1) business partners with DoDAAC partner mapping, (2) materials/service masters, (3) projects and WBS with status and budgets, (4) contract masters: Dassian contract with full CLIN/SLIN/ACRN tree and cumulative funding as of cutover, then the SD contract, (5) open billing plans and milestone status, (6) open AR at line level, (7) unbilled position by category (load as reconciled balances, never as a plug), (8) RAR legacy data transfer (POBs with inception-to-date revenue and cost so POC continues correctly), (9) in-flight items: open progress payment balances and unliquidated amounts, open PBP events, undefinitized actions. Cutover rule: bill everything billable in legacy before cutover; migrating an invoice mid-flight through WAWF is not worth it.

Migration gotchas learned the hard way:

- Cumulative funding must reconcile to the government's records (the mod history in EDA), not to the legacy system, because the legacy system is often already wrong; reconcile before load, not after.
- Progress payment unliquidated balances and PBP events in flight need explicit cutover treatment; if they are not loaded, the first post-go-live delivery invoice liquidates against nothing and overbills.
- RAR legacy transfer needs inception-to-date cost and revenue per POB; if legacy revenue was computed at contract level, the POB split is a management judgment that Revenue must sign before load.
- Provisional billing rate effectivity history must come across, or retroactive true-up billing after go-live becomes impossible to compute.
- Load a small pilot wave (three to five contracts spanning FFP, CPFF, T&M, and one UCA) through to a submitted test voucher before committing the full portfolio.

**CUI and export control:** conformed contracts, SOWs, CDRL content, and funding lines are routinely CUI (safeguarding per DFARS 252.204-7012 and NIST SP 800-171, with CMMC assessment applying); technical data attached to contracts on ITAR programs is export controlled, so contract document repositories need US-person access control even when the structured SAP data does not. Pricing and rate data is contractor proprietary and competition sensitive; treat rate decks as restricted even internally.

## Security and Authorizations

**PFCG role shapes** (names generic; build as single roles per shape, composite per job):

- Contract Display (broad): VA43, Dassian display, document display. The default for programs, finance, engineering.
- Contract Administrator: VA41/VA42 plus Dassian contract and mod maintenance, restricted by sales org/company code; no billing release, no pricing condition maintenance.
- Contract Mod Approver: workflow approval only (VP Contracts delegate); no create/change on the master.
- Pricing Maintainer: condition records and rate decks (VK11-shaped access plus Dassian rate tables); no contract create, no billing.
- Billing Specialist: VF01/VF04/VF02, DP91/DP96 execution, WAWF staging; no VF11 (cancellation) at junior tier, no contract change, no cash application.
- Billing Release / Certifying Official: release-to-accounting and portal release steps only.
- Revenue Accountant: RAR processing, RA execution (KKA2-shaped), POB maintenance under change control; no billing document creation.
- Cash Application (AR): incoming payments; no customer master maintenance, no billing.
- Auditor Display: display-only composite scoped to in-audit company codes, with S_TABU_DIS restricted to relevant table groups and no HR/payroll visibility.

**Segregation-of-duties toxic pairs:**

| Toxic pair | Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Create/change contract + create/release billing | Fabricated billable scope | Role split; weekly report of users touching both objects on same contract |
| Maintain pricing/rate conditions + create billing documents | Manipulated invoice values | Rate maintenance restricted to Pricing/Compliance; billing consumes rates read-only |
| Enter mod + approve mod | Unauthorized contract change | Workflow approval by separate role tied to delegation-of-authority matrix |
| Create billing + apply cash | Lapping / concealment | Classic AR split; enforce in GRC Access Control ruleset |
| Maintain provisional billing rates + submit vouchers | Overbilling the government | Compliance owns rate effectivity; billing cannot edit rate tables; quarterly rate-to-voucher reconciliation |
| Maintain POB/RAR config + post manual revenue journals | Revenue manipulation | POB changes under change control with Revenue Manager approval; manual revenue JEs flagged to Controller |
| Maintain customer/DoDAAC master + release invoices to portal | Misdirected payments | Master data maintenance in a shared-services role outside billing |

Mitigations run through SAP GRC Access Control (or equivalent) rulesets extended with the Dassian objects, plus firefighter IDs for emergencies with log review. **Auditor access design:** DCAA/DCMA normally receive documents and extracts, not system logons; where direct access is granted, use the Auditor Display role, time-boxed, scoped to the audited company codes, with usage logging, and pre-stage the standard pulls (voucher-to-timesheet trace, rate application, unbilled composition) so auditors are not exploring. **Export control specifics:** structured financial data is generally not ITAR, but attached technical data is; enforce US-person restriction at the DMS/content layer and on any role that opens contract attachments on controlled programs, and keep export-controlled documents out of general-purpose collaboration sites. On classified/SAP-restricted programs the entire contract may live in a separate enclave; the ERP then carries a sanitized shell record and the consultant should design for that pattern explicitly rather than discovering it in test.

**Unbilled decomposition** is this stream's signature analysis; every unbilled dollar must sit in a named category with a clearing mechanism:

| Category | Cause | Clears when |
|---|---|---|
| Rate variance | Costs accumulate at actual rates, vouchers bill at provisional billing rates | Annual rate true-up billing after final/settled rates |
| Fee withhold | FAR 52.216-8 withhold on fixed fee | Released at or near closeout per ACO |
| T&M retainage | FAR 52.232-7 payment withhold | Contract completion / final voucher |
| Over-ceiling / over-funding | Costs incurred beyond funded value or ceiling | Funding mod arrives, or write-off decision |
| Timing | Cost cutoff after the billing run; milestones or PBP events not yet achieved | Next billing cycle or event completion |
| In-dispute / suspended | DCAA Form 1 suspensions, questioned costs | Resolution with ACO/DCAA |

An unbilled balance that cannot be placed in this table is the finding; make the categorization a system output (Dassian BIL position vs PS actuals vs AR), not a quarterly spreadsheet heroic.

**Embedded vs warehouse:** operational reports (LOF burn, unbilled aging, billing status, mod aging) belong in embedded analytics: CDS views over ACDOCA, VBAK/VBAP, PRPS, and the Dassian tables, surfaced in Fiori, because they must reflect the live position. Blended executive reporting (bookings vs pipeline, backlog trend, DSO benchmarking) belongs in the warehouse layer (SAC, Snowflake/Databricks plus Power BI are all common) because it joins CRM pipeline, HR headcount, and ERP. Never compute funded backlog in the warehouse from re-derived logic; compute it once in the ERP/Dassian layer and replicate the result.

**KPIs the roles carry:** VP Contracts: definitization aging, mod cycle time, LOF notice timeliness. Billing lead: billing cycle time (cost cutoff to submitted voucher, world class is under 5 business days), WAWF first-pass yield, unbilled aging. Controller/CFO: DSO, unbilled over 90 days, backlog accuracy. Revenue Manager: EAC-to-revenue adjustment magnitude, close-cycle adherence. Pricing: win rate on priced bids, sweep findings disclosed vs found later.

## Role of AI

Concrete use cases with real leverage in this stream:

1. **Solicitation and contract shred:** extract Sections L and M requirements, CDRLs, and FAR/DFARS clauses from RFP or conformed contract PDFs into a compliance/flowdown matrix, cross-referenced to the clause library. Human CA validates before the matrix drives anything contractual.
2. **Mod triage and mod-to-master diff:** parse an SF30 from EDA, classify it (funding vs scope, unilateral vs bilateral), and draft the structured delta (CLIN/SLIN/ACRN changes) for the Dassian mod entry, with a side-by-side against the current contract master. This attacks the mod-sync problem directly.
3. **Unbilled root-cause classification:** categorize unbilled balances (rate variance, fee withhold, retainage, over-ceiling, funding gap, milestone timing) from billing, funding, and cost data, and draft the aging narrative the Controller owes the auditors.
4. **Voucher pre-submission QC:** check a draft SF1034/1035 or WAWF payload against provisional rate effectivity, CLIN/ACRN funding availability, period-of-performance dates, and prior cumulative billings; flag before submission rather than after rejection.
5. **LOF early warning narrative:** combine burn rate, commitments, and pending funding mods into a projected funds-exhaustion date per contract and draft the internal alert (the contractual notice itself remains a human-signed document).
6. **Proposal support:** BOE consistency checks against historical actuals (the CAS 401 consistency angle) and draft compliance matrices; also sweep support by surfacing vendor quotes and rate changes newer than the data in the cost volume.
7. **Closeout package assembly:** gather the contract file checklist (final DD250s, property, patents, final voucher prerequisites, rate settlement status) and draft the deobligation recommendation.

**Compliance boundary.** A human must certify anything the government relies on, because certification carries personal and corporate liability: the TINA certificate (Certificate of Current Cost or Pricing Data) is signed by a human after the sweep; payment requests carry a certifying official's signature and false submissions create False Claims Act exposure; LOF/LOC notices are contractual communications signed by the CA; revenue estimates (EAC judgments, loss provisions) are management's estimates under SOX, not a model's. AI drafts, assembles, and flags; it does not certify, sign, submit, or post.

**Grounding and auditability rules** for AI output that feeds government deliverables: every extracted value must carry a citation to source document, page, and version (which mod, which conformed copy); numbers destined for an invoice or certificate must be deterministically recomputed by the system of record, never transcribed from model output; prompts, retrieved context, and outputs are logged immutably so a defective pricing or false claims inquiry three years later can reconstruct what the tool showed the human; and the model may only retrieve from the controlled repository (EDA pulls, the contract file), never from uncontrolled copies. On ITAR programs the AI stack itself must satisfy the same US-person and infrastructure boundaries as the documents it reads.

## Operating Model

**Organization variants.** Primes run a program-aligned matrix: contracts managers and program finance sit with programs (often badged to the program's P&L) while reporting solid-line to the VP Contracts and Controller respectively, preserving independence on certifications and notices. Mid-tier and shared-services variants centralize government billing and revenue accounting in one team serving all programs (better control, worse program intimacy), with CAs still program-aligned. Small/Tier-2 suppliers combine contracts and billing under a single contracts-and-compliance manager. The stream's center of gravity: Contracts under GC/CEO, Pricing under CFO or BD, Billing and Revenue under the Controller, Compliance under the CFO; capture under BD. A consultant should diagram the actual delegation-of-authority matrix (who may sign what dollar value) in week one, because system workflow must mirror it.

**Calendar.**

- **Monthly:** cost cutoff, DP91/BIL billing runs and voucher submission, progress payment/PBP requests, EAC input lock then RAR/RAENH revenue run, unbilled reconciliation by category, backlog roll-forward (bookings, funding mods, revenue burn), LOF review, WAWF rejection cleanup.
- **Quarterly:** formal EAC reviews with program control (the Plan-to-Perform seam), loss provision assessment, RPO/backlog external disclosure support, definitization and closeout pipeline review, SoD/access review.
- **Annually:** incurred cost submission (ICE model) due six months after fiscal year end, provisional billing rate updates for the new year and retroactive rate true-up billing for the old one, forward pricing rate proposal (FPRP) support to Pricing, CAS disclosure statement maintenance, external audit walkthroughs of the O2C cycle, business system self-assessments.
- **Event-driven:** sweeps and certification at each qualifying negotiation, UCA definitization milestones, award-fee period submissions on CPAF work.

**Government counterparts:** the PCO (procuring contracting officer) owns the contract and signs mods; the ACO at DCMA administers it (progress payment approvals, rate agreements, business system determinations, closeout); DCMA specialists include price/cost analysts, industrial specialists, quality (source acceptance and DD250 signoff), and property administrators; corporate-level rate matters go through the CACO/DACO structure; DCAA audits (vouchers, ICS, TINA compliance, business system audits in support of DCMA determinations); DFAS (or the service equivalent) is the paying office behind WAWF. Know which counterpart owns which decision: rates are ACO decisions informed by DCAA audits, not DCAA decisions.

## How a Consultant Engages This Function

**Discovery: what to pull in the first two weeks.** The contract mix by type and by funded vs unfunded value (tells you the billing and revenue architecture before anyone opines); the ten largest contracts' conformed documents from the client's EDA pulls plus the CA's contract briefs (diff them: the gap is the mod-sync problem quantified); the unbilled trial balance by category and age; the definitization and closeout pipelines; the last DCAA voucher audit and business system correspondence; the WAWF rejection log for a quarter; and the delegation-of-authority matrix. This set lets you size the engagement honestly and spot the failure modes below before design starts.

**Workshop casting.** Contract structure workshop (CLIN/SLIN/ACRN modeling, IDIQ handling, mod process): VP Contracts, two working CAs with the messiest contracts, program finance, and the Dassian architect; explicitly walk one real UCA and one incrementally funded CPFF contract through the design. Billing workshop: billing lead, a specialist who clears WAWF rejections, Controller, Compliance for rates; test the design by regenerating three historical paid vouchers and one rejected one. Revenue workshop: Revenue Manager, program control (EAC owners), external-audit liaison; decide the RAR vs RAENH posting boundary and the POB granularity rule. Pricing/estimating workshop: Pricing Director, estimators, Compliance; map ProPricer structures to SAP cost elements and the CAS 401 consistency story. Never run a combined "O2C mega-workshop"; the billing people and the capture people share almost no vocabulary.

**Decision sign-off ladder:** POB granularity and revenue engine choice: CFO (with external auditor socialization). CLIN-to-WBS-to-POB structural alignment rule: VP Contracts plus Controller jointly (this single decision drives most downstream pain). Billing formats and portal routing: Controller with Compliance concurrence. Rate table ownership and effectivity governance: Compliance. Mod workflow and delegation limits: VP Contracts. Anything touching what is submitted to the government (voucher logic, DD250 content, progress payment calculation): validated against paid history and signed off by the certifying official, not just tested in QA.

**Common failure modes:** (1) modeling contracts in plain SD and bolting funding onto a Z-field, which collapses at the first multi-ACRN incremental funding mod; (2) letting mods be entered as direct edits to the contract master, destroying the audit trail and the conformed-contract tie-out; (3) designing POBs after go-live instead of aligning CLIN/WBS/POB at contract setup, forcing manual revenue allocation forever; (4) migrating unbilled as one plug balance, which guarantees an unexplainable number at the first audit; (5) treating WAWF as an afterthought and discovering DoDAAC/partner-function gaps in the first live billing week; (6) sequencing the close so revenue runs before EAC lock, producing monthly revenue restatements; (7) ignoring the definitization and closeout backlogs during design, then having no home for UCAs and physically complete contracts; (8) letting IT own the rate tables.

**What world class looks like:** one contract master (Dassian) from which SD, PS budgets, billing plans, funding, and POBs derive with no re-keying; mods entered as versioned mod objects within days of EDA posting, with an AI-assisted shred feeding the CA; touchless voucher generation with human certification only at release, WAWF first-pass yield above 95 percent; unbilled fully categorized and under 60 days aging except documented rate true-ups; LOF projections continuous rather than monthly; funded/unfunded backlog produced from the system at close plus one day and tying to the external RPO disclosure; a definitization and closeout pipeline actively burned down; and an audit posture where the DCAA voucher trace, the TINA sweep file, and the unbilled walk are pre-staged standard outputs rather than fire drills. The sign you are there: the VP Contracts trusts the system's funded value over the CA's spreadsheet, and the Controller's unbilled meeting takes thirty minutes.
